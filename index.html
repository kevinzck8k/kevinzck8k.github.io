About — the short version

I'm a second-year ECE student at the University of Toronto on the PEY co-op stream, working across low-level programming, digital and embedded systems, and production-scale hardware & networking environments.

My interests sit where software pressure meets hardware reality — FPGA development, digital logic and finite-state machine design, networked systems that need to hold up under load, and the cybersecurity layer that protects them. I'm drawn to problems that require both careful low-level implementation and a view of the bigger system.

I work comfortably with version control, simulation, and structured testing, and I enjoy the part of engineering where a careful diagram or a clean module turns a messy problem into a solvable one.

02

VGA Shooting Game on FPGA

2024

Digital design · Verilog · DE1-SoC

A 2D shooting game implemented in Verilog on the DE1-SoC platform, with VGA output and keyboard-driven control. Game behaviour is organized into modular finite-state machines for the player, enemies, and projectiles so timing and state transitions stay maintainable.

My contributions — End-to-end digital design from FSM modules to VGA timing, PS/2 keyboard integration, and on-chip memory planning, including MIF-based storage for sprites and backgrounds to fit device constraints.

  • Verilog
  • Intel DE1-SoC
  • VGA
  • PS/2
  • Quartus
